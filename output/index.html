Happy 13th Birthday to the Naval Carrier-Based Aviation Force!

海军舰载航空兵部队，13岁生日快乐！
China's naval carrier-based aviation force marked its 13th anniversary on May 10, 2025, with a PLA media retrospective cataloguing the force's progression from its formal establishment on the shores of Bohai Bay in 2013 through a series of declared firsts: the J-15's inaugural carrier landing aboard Liaoning in 2012, first anti-ship missile launch in 2016, first Western Pacific deployment, first dual-carrier formation exercise in October 2024, and—most significantly—the first catapult takeoff and arrested landing training aboard Fujian by the J-15T, J-35, and KJ-600 airborne early warning aircraft in 2025. The confirmation that all three next-generation carrier aircraft types have now conducted catapult operations aboard Fujian is the most operationally significant disclosure in the piece, indicating that Fujian's air wing integration is advancing across strike, multirole, and airborne early warning missions simultaneously, which directly bears on assessments of when the carrier will achieve meaningful operational readiness.

Reforming Training Concepts, Upgrading Training Methods, Reshaping the Training Landscape: New-Quality Combat Capabilities (新质战斗力) Accelerating Generation on the High-Altitude Front Line

革新训练理念 升级训练手段 重塑训练格局 新质战斗力在高原一线加速生成
A PLA Daily report describes concurrent training activities by Tibet Military District and Xinjiang Military District brigades, Rocket Force and Air Force units, and People's Armed Police Tibet General Corps elements on high-altitude plateau training grounds, emphasizing integration of UAVs, data links, robotic systems, and simulation technology under the rubric of 'new-quality combat capabilities.' The report signals a deliberate PLA effort to accelerate multi-domain, intelligentized training specifically adapted to high-altitude conditions along the Indian border, with the Gangbala Radar Station's shift to a remote duty mode and the normalization of joint exercises between ground and air units indicating incremental but concrete improvements in readiness and interoperability in the Western Theater. The piece is useful as evidence that the PLA is treating the plateau not as a logistical constraint to be managed but as a training environment to be exploited for systems-integration exercises.

Postal Routes Opened to Remote Outposts, Front-Line Personnel of the Information Support Force Enjoy Efficient Logistics

偏远点位开通邮路，信息支援部队一线官兵畅享高效物流
The PLA Information Support Force, in coordination with the State Post Bureau and China Post Group, has opened regular postal delivery routes to 13 previously inaccessible remote outposts—including the Tianshuihai and Hongshanhe Maintenance Stations on the Tibetan Plateau and sites in the northeast, northwest, and southwestern frontier—reducing average delivery times from seven days to under two days and achieving weekly or better delivery frequency at high-altitude and remote locations. The initiative is notable less for its welfare dimension than for what a force leader explicitly frames as a dual-use logistics objective: the civil-military coordination mechanisms, emergency transportation protocols, and planned logistics information platform are described as integrating peacetime and wartime functions, suggesting the ISF is using this effort to build last-mile sustainment infrastructure for its distributed technical outposts. For analysts tracking ISF basing and operational support, the named locations and the explicit 'peacetime-wartime integration' framing provide useful evidence on how the force is hardening logistics for its remote signals, communications, and maintenance nodes.

A PLA political commentary published under the 'Bayi Sharp Commentary' banner calls on Party member cadres to solicit feedback from rank-and-file officers and soldiers as the primary metric for evaluating political achievements, framing grassroots satisfaction as a corrective against superficial 'achievement projects' and 'image projects.' The piece is part of the PLA's ongoing study-and-education campaign on cultivating a 'correct view of political achievement' (正确政绩观), a Xi-directed initiative aimed at redirecting cadre incentives away from performative metrics toward measurable unit readiness and morale. The commentary signals continued institutional concern that careerism and formalism among mid-level officers remain a drag on grassroots combat effectiveness, a persistent theme in PLA political work that has intensified under Xi's broader military discipline campaign.
