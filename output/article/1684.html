Chinese Navy Task Force 83 Arrives in Russia for Friendly Visit

中国海军83舰编队抵达俄罗斯进行友好访问
PLA Daily (解放军报) 23 June 2026
View original source ↗
About this analysis
Summary
Chinese Navy Task Force 83, comprising the training ship Qi Jiguang and the amphibious dock landing ship Kunlunshan, arrived at Vladivostok on June 23 for a four-day port visit, carrying cadets from the PLA Naval University of Engineering and the Naval Submarine Academy on a distant-seas training mission. The pairing of a dedicated training vessel with a large amphibious platform extends the observable pattern of PLA Navy cadet cruises using operationally capable warships rather than purpose-built training hulls alone, giving midshipmen underway exposure on a ship class relevant to amphibious operations. The Russian Pacific Fleet's participation in the pier reception documents the current state of PLA-Russian Navy working-level engagement at Vladivostok, a port that has hosted prior Chinese naval visits and serves as a baseline reference point for tracking the tempo of bilateral naval access.

On the morning of June 23, the Chinese Navy ship Qi Jiguang arrived at the port of Vladivostok, Russia. (Xinhua/Andrei Matvienko)

Xinhua, Vladivostok, June 23 (Reporter Sun Ping) — Chinese Navy Task Force 83, currently executing a distant-seas training and visit mission (远海实习访问任务), arrived at the port of Vladivostok, Russia on the 23rd, beginning a four-day friendly visit to Russia.

On the morning of the 23rd local time, the Chinese Navy ships Qi Jiguang and Kunlunshan arrived at the port of Vladivostok. More than 200 people came to the pier to welcome them, including staff from the Chinese Consulate General in Vladivostok, representatives of local overseas Chinese, representatives of Chinese students studying abroad, and representatives of officers and soldiers of the Russian Navy's Pacific Fleet. Cadets from the PLA Naval University of Engineering and the Naval Submarine Academy lined up by sections on the deck to greet the welcoming crowd.
