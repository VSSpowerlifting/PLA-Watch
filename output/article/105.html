[Bilingual] China and Thailand to Hold 'Strike-2026' Joint Army Training

【双语】中泰将举行“突击-2026”陆军联训
PLA Daily (解放军报) 9 May 2026
View original source ↗
About this analysis
Summary
The PLA and Royal Thai Army will conduct their eighth iteration of the bilateral 'Strike' joint training exercise in Thailand in late May 2025, focusing on mountain and jungle counter-terrorism operations with mixed teams training in combat casualty care, unmanned equipment operation, and live-fire shooting. The exercise continues an established pattern of PLA Army engagement with Southeast Asian partners under a counter-terrorism framework and provides incremental evidence of PLA efforts to build interoperability with ASEAN militaries, though the announcement contains no new doctrinal or capability signals beyond prior iterations.
Translation

On the afternoon of May 9, Senior Colonel Jiang Bin, Deputy Director of the Ministry of National Defense News Bureau and Ministry of National Defense Spokesperson, released information on recent military-related matters.

Jiang Bin: In mid-to-late May, the China-Thailand "Strike-2026" joint army training will be held in Thailand. This joint training focuses on the subject of joint mountain and jungle counter-terrorism operations, with both sides forming mixed teams to conduct training in subjects including combat casualty care, unmanned equipment operation, and live-fire shooting. This is the 8th time the two countries' armies have held this series of joint training, aimed at continuously deepening bilateral friendship and cooperation and enhancing joint counter-terrorism operational capabilities.

Jiang Bin: China-Thailand Strike 2026 joint army training will be held in Thailand in mid-and-late May. Focusing on the theme of Joint Counter-terrorism Operations in Mountains and Jungles, Chinese and Thai troops will form mixed teams to train on such subjects as combat casualty care, operating procedures for unmanned equipment, and live-fire shooting. Strike 2026 will be the eighth iteration of its kind between the two armies, which aims to continuously deepen bilateral friendship and cooperation, and enhance the troops' capabilities in joint counter-terrorism operations.
