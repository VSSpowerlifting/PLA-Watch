Ministry of National Defense: In 2026, Military Academies Plan to Enroll Approximately 17,500 Ordinary Senior High School Graduates Army-Wide

国防部：2026年全军院校计划招收约1.75万名普通高中毕业生
PLA Daily (解放军报) 9 June 2026
View original source ↗
About this analysis
Summary
At a June 9 press briefing, MND spokesperson Zhang Xiaogang announced that 22 PLA military academies plan to enroll approximately 17,500 senior high school graduates in 2026, with three structural changes: a new integrated commissioned-officer cultivation pathway, two new majors at NUDT specifically to grow the political officer corps, and a '5+3' undergraduate-to-master's track at the Army, Navy, and Air Force medical universities. The explicit framing of enrollment around 'deepening political rectification and consolidation' documents continued institutional pressure to rebuild the officer pipeline on ideologically vetted terms following the Rocket Force purge — the NUDT political officer track in particular extends the pattern of sourcing ideological cadres from within the technical university system rather than relying on existing political academies. The 5+3 medical track is a structural reform worth tracking as a baseline for how the PLA is professionalizing its medical support force ahead of potential large-scale contingency demands.
Translation

On the afternoon of June 9, Ministry of National Defense spokesperson Senior Colonel Zhang Xiaogang released information on recent military-related matters.

Reporter: The 2026 national college entrance examination is currently underway. Please introduce the plan for military academies to enroll ordinary senior high school graduates.

Zhang Xiaogang: In 2026, 22 academies across the entire military plan to enroll approximately 17,500 ordinary senior high school graduates. The military academy enrollment work fully implements the requirements of deepening political rectification and consolidation (深化政治整训), precisely aligns with the personnel needs of units for combat, construction, readiness, and governance (战建备治), and scientifically formulates enrollment plans in accordance with the new planning for military academy education and training tasks. There are three main characteristics:

First, deployment of an integrated cultivation pathway for growing commissioned officers (生长军官贯通培养): during the undergraduate education phase, students will build a solid foundation in scientific culture, military-political quality, and professional fundamentals, with emphasis on cultivating command literacy (指挥素养), consolidating long-term development potential, and laying a solid foundation for pre-assignment training in units.

Second, strengthening targeted cultivation of political officers: relying on the National University of Defense Technology, two majors—Marxist Theory and Ideological-Political Education—will be opened to enroll young students, so as to consolidate and strengthen our military's political officer corps from the source.

Third, carrying out integrated "5+3" cultivation in clinical medicine: beginning this year, at the Army, Navy, and Air Force's three military medical universities, an integrated cultivation system will be established linking five years of undergraduate medical education with three years of master's professional degree graduate education and standardized residency training, in order to cultivate and forge a high-quality, specialized medical support and healthcare force (卫勤医疗队伍).

In addition, relying on the distinctive and advantageous disciplines of the academies, talent cultivation in emerging technology fields will continue.

Youth tempers the sword; building a strong military, I am part of it (青春砺剑，强军有我). We look forward to more aspiring young people applying to military academies, singing the song of youth in the vibrant military camp, and injecting inexhaustible momentum into accelerating the building of a world-class military. Candidates and parents are asked to follow the official enrollment announcements of military academies for further information, and to remain vigilant against online enrollment fraud and false information.
