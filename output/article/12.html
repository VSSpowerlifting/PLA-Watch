First-Instance Verdicts Handed Down in the Cases of Wei Fenghe and Li Shangfu

魏凤和案、李尚福案一审宣判
PLA Daily (解放军报) 7 May 2026
View original source ↗
Summary
On May 7, 2026, Chinese military courts handed down first-instance verdicts against Wei Fenghe and Li Shangfu—consecutive former Ministers of National Defense and Central Military Commission members—sentencing both to death with a two-year reprieve, permanent deprivation of political rights, and full asset confiscation, with automatic commutation to life imprisonment without possibility of reduction or parole. The verdicts mark the formal judicial conclusion of the most significant corruption prosecutions within the PLA's senior leadership in the Xi era, targeting two consecutive defense ministers and signaling that CMC-level rank provides no immunity from terminal career and legal consequences. The identical sentencing structure applied to both men, and the explicit bar on sentence reduction or parole, reflects a deliberate deterrence signal directed at the officer corps rather than a case-specific proportionality judgment.
First-instance verdicts against two consecutive former Defense Ministers and CMC members — Wei Fenghe and Li Shangfu — both receiving suspended death sentences, representing an unprecedented public accountability action at the highest level of PLA civilian leadership and signaling the scale of the corruption investigation within the defense establishment.
Translation

Xinhua News Agency, Beijing, May 7 — On May 7, 2026, a military court lawfully rendered a verdict in the bribery case against Wei Fenghe, former member of the Central Military Commission and former State Councilor and Minister of National Defense. The court found Wei Fenghe guilty of the crime of accepting bribes and sentenced him to death with a two-year reprieve, deprivation of political rights for life, and confiscation of all personal assets. Upon the lawful commutation of his death sentence with reprieve to life imprisonment at the expiration of the two-year period, he shall be subject to life imprisonment without the possibility of sentence reduction or parole.

On May 7, 2026, a military court lawfully rendered a verdict in the bribery and bribe-giving case against Li Shangfu, former member of the Central Military Commission and former State Councilor and Minister of National Defense. The court found Li Shangfu guilty of the crime of accepting bribes and the crime of offering bribes, and, applying combined punishment for multiple offenses, determined that the sentence to be carried out shall be death with a two-year reprieve, deprivation of political rights for life, and confiscation of all personal assets. Upon the lawful commutation of his death sentence with reprieve to life imprisonment at the expiration of the two-year period, he shall be subject to life imprisonment without the possibility of sentence reduction or parole.
