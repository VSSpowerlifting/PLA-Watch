Southern Theater Command Spokesperson Zhai Shichen Issues Statement on Provocative Actions by Dutch Warship

南部战区新闻发言人翟士臣就荷兰军舰侵权行为发表谈话
PLA Daily (解放军报) 27 May 2026

About this analysis
Summary
On May 27, the Southern Theater Command deployed naval and air forces to expel Dutch Navy frigate De Ruyter after it transited the Xisha Islands, with its helicopter conducting multiple flights the PLA characterized as violations of Chinese territorial airspace; the response included voice warnings and electronic jamming. Spokesperson Zhai Shichen's statement documents the PLA's use of electronic warfare as a declared, routine expulsion tool against a NATO-member vessel—not merely a surveillance or deterrence measure—raising the question of how Beijing intends to normalize jamming as a legal enforcement action in disputed airspace. The Xisha transit extends a pattern of European naval freedom-of-navigation operations in the South China Sea and confirms the Southern Theater Command's standing posture of treating such transits as sovereignty violations requiring active countermeasures rather than diplomatic protest alone.
PLA Southern Theater Command explicitly acknowledges use of electronic jamming against a NATO member warship (Dutch frigate De Ruyter) near the Paracel Islands, a rare public admission of an offensive electronic warfare measure employed against a European navy conducting a freedom of navigation operation.
Translation

Southern Theater Command Spokesperson Zhai Shichen, Naval Senior Captain, stated that on May 27, the Dutch Navy frigate De Ruyter illegally intruded into China's Xisha Islands, with its shipborne helicopter making multiple sorties in violation of China's territorial airspace. The People's Liberation Army Southern Theater Command organized naval and air forces to take necessary measures in accordance with laws and regulations, including voice warnings and warning electronic jamming, to expel the vessel. The Dutch side's actions seriously infringed upon China's territorial sovereignty and maritime and aerial security, seriously violated international law and the basic norms of international relations, seriously undermined peace and stability in the South China Sea, and are highly prone to causing misunderstandings and miscalculations. We resolutely oppose this and sternly warn the Dutch side to immediately cease its infringing and provocative actions. Theater command forces remain on high alert at all times and will resolutely defend national sovereignty and security as well as regional peace and stability.
