Set Sail! China's Navy Task Force 83 Departs to Execute Far-Sea Comprehensive Training and Goodwill Visit Mission

起航！中国海军83舰编队执行远海综合实习访问任务
PLA Daily (解放军报) 16 June 2026
View original source ↗
About this analysis
Summary
On June 15, PLAN Task Force 83 — comprising the training ship Qi Jiguang and the amphibious transport dock Kunlunshan — departed Qingdao carrying over 400 naval cadets from the Naval University of Engineering and the Naval Submarine Academy, along with 20 foreign military cadets from Brazil, Myanmar, and other countries, for a far-sea training and goodwill visit mission. The inclusion of foreign cadets in a structured, multi-module shipboard curriculum extending into overseas port calls documents the PLAN's use of cadet training voyages as a dual-purpose instrument: building professional competence while cultivating defense relationships with partner navies in the Global South. This fits the established pattern of Qi Jiguang deployments as soft-power platforms, but the explicit integration of foreign cadets into the training syllabus — rather than treating them as observers — extends that pattern toward a more formalized military education exchange function.
Translation

Task Force 83, composed of the PLA Navy vessels Qi Jiguang and Kunlunshan, sets sail — Chinese and foreign cadets are integrated and trained together to execute a far-sea comprehensive training and goodwill visit mission.

Qi Jiguang departs the pier. Photo by Luo Zhenyu.

PLA Daily, Qingdao, June 15 — Reported by Zheng Hongsheng and correspondent Xu Lin: On June 15, Task Force 83, composed of the Chinese Navy vessels Qi Jiguang and Kunlunshan, cast off from a military port in Qingdao, Shandong, carrying more than 400 naval cadets, and set sail to execute a far-sea comprehensive training and goodwill visit mission.

According to available information, the cadets participating in this shipboard training and visit are drawn primarily from the Naval University of Engineering and the Naval Submarine Academy. Notably, 20 foreign military cadets from countries including Brazil and Myanmar will be integrated with Chinese cadets for joint training to complete this mission together.

This far-sea comprehensive training and goodwill visit mission is aimed at comprehensively improving cadets' naval professional competence and their ability to fulfill future assigned duties. During the transit, the task force will systematically organize cadets to conduct teaching and training across five modules: terrestrial navigation (地文航海), celestial navigation (天文航海), common subjects (共同科目), professional fundamentals (专业基础), and on-the-job observation (岗位见习). At the same time, special lectures on on-site instruction (现地教学) and professional enrichment (专业拓展) will be organized in accordance with the characteristics of the sea areas transited.

During port calls at foreign ports, the task force plans to hold deck receptions and observation visits, strengthen exchanges, mutual learning, and practical cooperation with the navies of host countries, and contribute to serving the building of a maritime community with a shared future (海洋命运共同体).
